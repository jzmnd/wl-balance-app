    Clustering of Activity Times

    Jeremy Smith

    Using K-Means clustering we can group different people by how they spend their time.

    These clusters do not assume any demographic information about the respondents but we are able to see different patterns of time use. For example, some clusters are obviously part-time workers while others are full-time, students appear in one cluster and we can see whether a group has children or not. The radius of the bubble in the diagram below indicates the average time spent on that activity for the particular group.
