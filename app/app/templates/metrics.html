    How Do We Measure Work Life Balance?

    Jeremy Smith

    We need to define some suitable metrics for work-life balance. I have chosen 5 metrics that can be easily calculated from activity times that give an indication of a person's work-life balance. The first 3 are scores - the higher the better. The last two classify groups by a daily cut-off time, first for paid work and second for unpaid work (e.g. housework, childcare).

    Metric 1

    Weighted difference between "life activities" and "paid work activities".
    This metric is based on several key activity times related to work-life balance and scaled to be a score between 0 and 1.

    Metric 2

    Weighted difference between "life activities" and "unpaid work activities".
    As for Metric 1 but with unpaid rather than paid work activities.

    Metric 3

    Percentage of day spent on Personal Care.
    Here we simply calculate the time spent on personal care activities (including sleep) as a percentage of the day.

    Metric 4

    Percentage of day spent on Leisure/Socializing.
    Here we simply calculate time spent on leisure, social or relaxation activities as a percentage of the day.

    Metric 5

    More than 9 hours of work in the day?
    This categorizes a respondent by whether they work a "long" day or not.

    Metric 6

    More than 4 hours of unpaid work in the day?
    This categorizes a respondent by whether they spend a significant amount of time on childcare, housework etc.
