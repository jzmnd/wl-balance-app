    Background to the Project

    Jeremy Smith

    The OECD publishes worldwide statistics on quality of work-life balance by country. As we can see, the US falls relatively low on this scale, and so it is certainly something that should be taken seriously by the healthcare industry, and something that is valuable for them to quantify.

    I am using data from the American Time Use Survey (ATUS) that is published by the US Bureau for Labor Statistics. This dataset has daily activity times for respondents between 2003 and 2015 as well as their sociodemographic information.

    If we simply pick out some key activity times from the ATUS dataset and plot their deviation from mean as a function of different demographic groups we can see that there are some trends that will form the basis of our modeling. These plots are shown for education level, weekly income, and age group.
